Basic Drill and Cerimonial

Basic Drill and Cerimonial (BDCC) is a three week camp usually held at the Cadet Training Center in Valcartier, Quebec. The first two weeks are spent learning many important leadership skills as well as sharpening your overall drill. The weekends are generally spent doing fun things such as going to amusement parks, having dances and different team building activities. You will also have a significant amount of free time where you will be able to socialize and make new friends. The last week of this course is used to evaluate what you have learned by having you actually take part in assisting with a flight. Final parade will happen on the last day. This is where the most extraordinary cadets will be recognized and BDCC cadets will get a chance to lead flights and demonstrate what they have learned. In addition to the course material itself, the cadets will have a chance to be a part of the base-wide drill team. This team consits of the best drill cadets from all the courses at Valcartier. Being selected for the drill team at BDCC will prove as a big asset when trying out for the 75 Drill Team in the coming years. The Basic Drill and Cerimonial course is an exciting and interactive way to explore deeper into drill and leadership while also having a fun time!

Drill and Cerimonial Instructor

The Drill and Cerimonial Instrucotr Course (DCIC) is a six week camp that is held at the Cadet Training Center in Greenwood, Nova Scotia. It is similar but also drastically different from the basic course. Although you do spend around a week or two learning more about leadership, most of your time will be spent learning differnt types of drill such as flag drill, rifle drill and sword drill. Your evaluation for this course will be done in the final week, when you will be asked to play the role of a flight commander for a day. This shows your leadership skills when conducting the flight as well as your drill knowledge when moving the flight around the base. Final parade and the drill team will be very similar to the ones of BDCC. This course will help you make life long friends and help you grow tremendously. Another benefit is that you will have an upper hand when going through the 75 Drill Team tryouts, as you will know more overall drill movements (flag, rifle and sword) than most of the other people trying out. You will have also spent the whole summer learning and doing drill. In conclusion the Drill and Cerimonial Instrucotr Course is a uniqe experience that will be very advantageous for your drill life.
